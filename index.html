Hi, I’m Markus!

I’m a Research Scientist in AI at the Norwegian Defence Research Establishment (FFI), where I work on the hard edge of robotics, robot dynamics, and control systems. My research sits at the crossroads of autonomous decision-making and physical machines — from underwater vehicles and maritime drones to industrial manipulators and aerial platforms. Over the past two decades I’ve moved between heavy industry and leading academic labs, and that combination has shaped how I think: I care as much about a clean control law as I do about getting it to run reliably on a real robot in a real environment.

My work spans AI-driven planning, distributed autonomous agents, reinforcement learning, nonlinear control, and full-stack robotic software in ROS 2 and Linux. I enjoy building systems from first principles — deriving dynamics, designing estimators and controllers, and then closing the loop with perception, learning, and operator interaction. I also spend a lot of time mentoring PhD and MSc students, because translating complex ideas into the next generation of roboticists is something I genuinely love.

Outside the lab, I’m usually on a gravel bike, exploring trails and clearing my head. That mix of deep technical work and outdoor endurance suits me — robotics research is a long game, and I’m comfortable putting in the miles.

Take a look at my code and open-source work, browse my industrial and robotics portfolio, or dive into my latest research and publications. If you’re interested in collaboration, PhD or MSc supervision, or just want to talk robotics and control theory, drop me an email — I’m always happy to connect.
